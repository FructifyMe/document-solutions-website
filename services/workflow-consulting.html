When to call us

Five signals you've outgrown "just hit print."

If two or more of these sound like your office, the savings from workflow redesign usually outpace anything you'd save by negotiating a copier lease.

Sensitive print sits at the tray

Confidential documents — HR letters, legal filings, financial statements — get printed and forgotten. Anyone walking past picks them up.

You can't bill print to clients

Law firms and consultants need per-matter or per-client cost recovery. Without it, every page is a write-off.

Scan-to-email is your only option

Files end up in inboxes instead of where they belong — SharePoint, OneDrive, the EHR, the case management system, the cloud storage your team actually uses.

Scanned PDFs aren't searchable

Old contracts, signed agreements, patient records — scanned, archived, and effectively invisible because they're images, not text.

You don't know who's printing what

No per-user accounting means no way to see why your color volume tripled last quarter, or whether department X is over-printing.

Compliance auditors keep asking

HIPAA, SOX, GLBA, state privacy laws — they all want a print/scan audit trail. uniFLOW gives you one out of the box.

What we deploy

Canon uniFLOW, configured around your business.

We're not a generalist consulting shop. We deploy uniFLOW, end-to-end, on Canon hardware we already service. That focus is why our engagements ship in weeks, not quarters.

Secure print release

Print jobs hold in a secure queue until the user authenticates at the device with a badge, PIN, or login. Nothing sits unattended.

Scan-to-cloud workflows

One-touch scan straight to SharePoint, OneDrive, Google Drive, Dropbox, network folders, or your EHR — with naming rules, OCR, and metadata automatically applied.

OCR & searchable PDFs

Every scanned document becomes searchable text. Five years of paper archives turn into a queryable library.

Per-user / per-matter accounting

See exactly who printed what, when, and at what cost. Bill it back to the matter, the department, or the cost center.

Active Directory / SSO

Single sign-on from Active Directory or Azure AD. New employees get print access automatically; departed employees lose it the moment HR offboards them.

Compliance & audit trails

Full logs of every print, copy, scan, and fax — sortable by user, device, time, and document. Hand them to your compliance officer and move on.

Common questions

Workflow consulting, plain language.

What is uniFLOW?
uniFLOW is Canon's enterprise print management software. It adds secure print release, per-user accounting, scan-to-cloud, OCR, and routing rules across an entire fleet of devices. We deploy uniFLOW as the technical backbone of every workflow consulting engagement.
Who actually needs document workflow consulting?
The biggest fits: law firms with confidentiality and per-matter cost recovery requirements; medical and dental practices that scan to EHR systems; financial services firms with print accounting and compliance needs; and any organization that's outgrown the "send the file to the printer" approach.
How is NJM Consulting different from a regular dealer service?
NJM Consulting is the consulting practice within Document Solutions. We start by mapping how documents actually move through your business — current state — before recommending any hardware or software. The deliverable is a workflow design and implementation plan, not a copier quote.
How long does it take?
Most engagements run 4-8 weeks from kickoff to go-live, depending on the size of your fleet and integration complexity. Discovery is typically 1-2 weeks, design and quoting another 1-2 weeks, and deployment 2-4 weeks.
Will it integrate with our existing systems?
uniFLOW integrates with Active Directory and Azure AD for authentication; with SharePoint, OneDrive, Google Drive, Dropbox, and network folders for scan destinations; with most EHR systems via standard protocols; and with major accounting platforms for chargebacks. We scope the integrations during discovery.
What does it cost?
Engagements price by scope rather than headcount. A typical small-business deployment (one office, 5–15 devices) runs $4,000–$12,000 in consulting plus uniFLOW licensing. Larger or multi-site deployments scale from there. We quote firmly after discovery, not before.
