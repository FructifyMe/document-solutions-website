Common questions

What North Shore businesses ask us first.

What areas does Document Solutions serve?
We serve the North Shore of Massachusetts from our Beverly office, including Salem, Danvers, Peabody, Gloucester, Marblehead, Manchester-by-the-Sea, Newburyport, and the surrounding communities along the Beverly-to-Boston corridor.
Are you a Canon authorized dealer?
Yes. Document Solutions is a Canon authorized dealer with factory-trained technicians for the full Canon imageRUNNER and imageCLASS lines. We carry current Canon promotions, trade-in values, and dealer pricing — all of it passed through transparently in your proposal.
How is "managed print" different from just buying a copier?
A managed print agreement bundles the hardware, supplies (toner, drums), automatic replenishment, remote monitoring, and on-site service into a single per-page price. You stop buying toner reactively and you stop calling for service after a breakdown — the system flags it for us first. We'll quote you against your current spend during a free assessment so you can see the difference for yourself.
What is uniFLOW and why would I need it?
uniFLOW is Canon's print management platform. We deploy it for clients who want secure print release (jobs only print when the user authenticates at the device), per-user or per-department accounting, scan-to-cloud workflows, and OCR for searchable PDF archives. This is the consulting side of our business — branded NJM Consulting — and it's where the biggest workflow gains usually live, especially in law, medical, and financial services offices.
Can you take over a lease that's still active?
Yes. We routinely transition customers from competitors and structure new agreements 90–120 days ahead of an expiring lease so there's no service gap. If your current lease is mid-term, we can help model an early-buyout to see whether the math works — sometimes it does, sometimes it doesn't, and we'll tell you straight.
How fast can you respond to a service call?
Service is dispatched out of our Beverly office. Because our technicians are Canon factory-trained on the equipment we sell, we typically arrive with the right parts the first time. For specific response-time commitments, ask us during your assessment — we'll set expectations based on your equipment and location.
What does an assessment actually involve?
It's a 30-minute conversation, in person or over the phone, where Jim or one of the team walks through your current devices, monthly volumes, supply costs, service issues, and lease end dates. There's no commitment. At the end you get a written recap and, if it makes sense, a three-tier proposal you can compare to what you're spending today.
